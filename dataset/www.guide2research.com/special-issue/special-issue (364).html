Special Issue Call for Papers:

We propose the development of a special issue that focuses on theories and practices of digital government in China in the last two decades to mark the official launch of China Chapter of the Digital Government Society. This special issue is inspired primarily by two motivations. First, digital government has experienced rapid development in China thanks to policy promotion, public and private investment, as well as technological advancements. These rapid developments are in sync with the rest of the world, and contribute to the collective growth of knowledge base of the global digital government theory and practices. This knowledge could provide references to other countries, especially emerging or transitional countries, to draw lessons by comparing the similarities and differences in policies, strategies, and implementation of digital government. Furthermore, the conditions under which lessons learned from China could be transferred to other countries should be also examined. Second, due to the unparalleled scale of digital government undertakings as well as the unique political, social, economic, and cultural context of China, additional layers of complexity and challenges associated with digital government emerged and needed to be addressed. Some managerial, social and political experiments in China with digital means are especially intriguing and can be valuable sources of insights to be investigated by scholars.

We anticipate that this special issue could serve as both a systematic survey and a review of the practices of digital government in China in the past, as well as a launching pad for inspiring ideas and new knowledge to be discovered for the global digital government community. In the following sections, we discuss the background, objectives, and timeline of this special issue.

BACKGROUND:

In the past decade, digital innovations fostered by emerging technologies including Big Data, Cloud Computing, Social Media, Internet of Things (IoT) and Artificial intelligence, has created a new context for public administration and governance in China (Liang & Jin, 2013; Zheng, 2013; Zeng, 2016; Hou, 2017). Governments at all levels in China have made heavy investment to adopting new technologies in the efforts to promote performance, service quality and citizen engagement (Song et al., 2015; Medaglia & Zhu, 2017; Jiang et al., 2017). For example, data-driven governance has enhanced public needs identification, policy formulation and implementation, and services delivery; open data initiatives increasingly release the potential of government data for innovations and entrepreneurship (Zheng & Gao, 2016); Artificial Intelligence technologies have been adopted to improve operation efficiency and services quality; Big data, social media, mobile technologies and IoT have brought new approaches of responding to public health emergences and fostering collaborations among government and various stakeholders in crisis. During the most recent public health emergency—the outbreak of the coronavirus disease (COVID-19), digital technologies have been broadly deployed by Chinese government and hospitals for medical consultation, patient tracking, patient quarantine, tracking of contacts, border controls, policy education, and disclosure of information.

However, the relationship between technologies, public administration and sustainable socio-economic development remain complex, controversial and ambiguous, and the rapid development in technologies have not resolved or alleviated some of the long-standing policy challenges that government is expected to address. For instances, the obstacles impeding the adoption of new technologies, both inside and outside, have not been fully identified; the impact of social media on the contribution to the interactivity between government and citizens (Hao et al., 2016) has not been fully understood yet; the question why big data technologies have not transformed public policy-makings as expected (van der Voort et al., 2019) demands a convincing answer; what measures should be taken to address digital divide and to develop adaptive governance to better deal with the complex and uncertain environment (Janssen & van der Voort, 2016; Wang et al., 2018) remain unclear. In general, how government could better apply digital technologies in governance processes and adapt itself in the changing technological environment are demanding high level of attention from scholars and practitioners.

Moreover, the study of digital government is dependent on the understanding of the context (Janowski, 2015; Zheng et al., 2013). The context of digital government in China is especially intriguing as China exhibits characteristics of both a developed and a developing country. On the one hand, it faces issues common to developing or under-developed countries. Chinese governments play multi-facet roles, ranging from economic development, regulation, service provision to policy making and implementation, with a multi-layered system where local governments’ adoption and use of technologies are under the direction and guidance from central and provincial governments (Liang, et al., 2017). How China addresses the positive and negative aspects of large-scale and system-wide deployment of digital technologies to serve its society and support the functioning of a country with huge and diverse population could provide lessons for other developing countries and regions. On the other hand, China has the potential to emerge to be a leader in technological and social experiments, such as the adoption of Big Data and AI technologies and the role of government in integrating technological capabilities into governance capacity. Therefore, China presents a unique context to investigate new analytical perspectives and theoretical frameworks across different development stages.

OBJECTIVES OF THE SPECIAL ISSUE:

This special issue aims to review and examine digital government in China in the past two decades to inspire discussions and theory building among the global digital government research community. More specifically, the special issue intends to investigate the context and nature of digital government in China to provide a comprehensive, in-depth and balanced perspective. In particular, we attempt to collect studies to identify determinants, impacts and frameworks in a systematic and rigorous fashion to better understand the underlying mechanisms of digital government development in China.

RECOMMENDED TOPICS:

Topics to be discussed in this special issue include (but are not limited to) the following:

  • Digital transformation of Chinese government at the central and local level
  • Technological, managerial and policy experiments with digital means in the public sector in China
  • Sustainability of digital government initiatives in China
  • Adoption of advanced digital technologies in public services delivery, citizen engagement, public health emergence response in China
  • Government data governance, sharing and opening up in China
  • Data-driven policy making, implementation and evaluation in China
  • Artificial Intelligence in the public sector in China
  • Privacy, security, and digital divide issues in China
  • Emergency and pandemics response with digital technologies in China
  • Underlying mechanisms in developing digital government in China
  • Critical examination and future trends analysis of digital government in China
  • Comparative studies at global, national and city level related between China and other countries
